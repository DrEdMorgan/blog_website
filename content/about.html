Whether its making a simple map or running complex analyses on satellite data, students and researchers are often called upon to do some sort of geospatial analysis. 🗺

Fortunately, there are a wealth of tools available to do this, think R, Python and QGIS.🔨

Unfortunately, a steep learning curve is often required to apply these tools, particularly as we don’t all have a strong background in coding, GIS or remote sensing.

The UQ Geospatial Analysis Community of Practice (sometimes UQGAC) tries to make this process a bit less painful, by sharing our experiences, skills and tricks in a friendly and inclusive environment.

👩‍💻 We run monthly skill sharing tutorial sessions where a member of the community teaches us something they have learned along the way.

This is usually on the last thursday of each month.
See upcoming events for the next session.

And check out our blog for previous tutorials.
